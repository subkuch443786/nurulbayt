Our Story

About NurulBayt

We are a team of Muslim parents and educators dedicated to helping families raise righteous children through authentic Quran and Sunnah based guidance.

Who We Are

Born From a Parent's Prayer

NurulBayt began with a simple, sincere dua — the same one Prophet Ibrahim ﷺ made: "My Lord, grant me righteous children." We are Muslim parents who felt the weight of raising children in a modern world while staying true to Islamic values.

We looked for practical, authentic Islamic parenting guidance and found that most resources were either too academic, too vague, or simply not rooted in the Quran and Sunnah. So we built what we wished had existed when we needed it most.

NurulBayt — meaning "The Light of the Home" — is our commitment to bringing that light into every Muslim family's home, one article, one dua, one conversation at a time.

2024 Founded with a dua

Our Core Values

Everything we publish is guided by these three principles.

📖

Quran & Sunnah First

Every article is rooted in authentic Islamic sources. We never publish advice that contradicts the Quran or the Sunnah of the Prophet ﷺ.

💡

Practical & Actionable

We believe guidance must be usable. Every article gives parents real steps they can take today — not just theory, but lived practice.

❤️

Love Over Fear

We teach parents to raise children who love Allah — not out of fear or obligation, but out of genuine faith, gratitude, and connection.

Our Commitments to You

What every Muslim family can expect from NurulBayt.

1

Always Free

All NurulBayt articles and guidance will always be completely free. Islamic parenting knowledge should be accessible to every Muslim family, everywhere.

2

Authentic Sources Only

We only publish content rooted in the Quran, authentic Hadith, and trusted Islamic scholarship. No fabricated hadith, no cultural opinion presented as religion.

3

Practical for Modern Families

We understand the challenges Muslim families face today — screens, peer pressure, identity. Our guidance meets you where you are, not where we wish you were.

4

Respectful of All Backgrounds

Muslim families come from every culture and background. We write for all of them — with respect, inclusion, and a shared love of Islam as our foundation.
