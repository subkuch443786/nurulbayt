Teaching Children the 99 Names of Allah

By NurulBayt · 7 min read

The 99 names of Allah (Asma ul Husna) are a beautiful way to help children know and love their Creator. Each name reveals a unique attribute of Allah, making Him real and close to their hearts.

Here are practical, fun ways to teach the names of Allah to your children.

1. Start with Simple Names

Begin with names children can easily understand: Ar-Rahman (The Most Merciful), Ar-Razzaq (The Provider), Al-Wadud (The Loving).

💡 Tip: Say "Allah is Ar-Rahman, He loves us" in daily conversation.

2. Use Flashcards and Posters

Visual aids help children remember. Create or print colorful flashcards with one name and its meaning.

3. Sing the Names

Turn the names into a simple song or rhyme. Children learn faster through music and rhythm.

If you want to learn more about building confidence, read our guide on Building Confidence in Muslim Children.

4. Connect Names to Daily Life

When they eat, say "Alhamdulillah" for Ar-Razzaq. When they see the moon, say "SubhanAllah" for Al-Khaliq (The Creator).

5. Play Matching Games

Match the name to its meaning. For example: "Al-Ghafoor" means "The Forgiving."

🤲 Dua: "Allahumma inni as'aluka bi-asmika al-husna..." — "O Allah, I ask You by Your beautiful names..."

Final Thoughts

Teaching the 99 names of Allah is a journey of love and connection. Start small, be consistent, and make it joyful. May your children grow up knowing Allah by His beautiful names, in sha Allah.
