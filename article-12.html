How to Make Dua Fun for Kids

By NurulBayt · 6 min read

Dua is a beautiful gift from Allah — a direct connection between us and our Creator. But how do we make dua something children look forward to, not just a routine?

Here are practical, fun ideas to help your child love making dua.

1. Use Dua Cards

Write simple duas on colorful cards. Let your child pick one each day and read it together.

💡 Tip: Create a "Dua of the Day" ritual.

2. Make a Dua Jar

Write different duas on small pieces of paper and put them in a jar. Each day, pick one and make dua together.

3. Connect Dua to Emotions

Teach them to make dua when they're happy, sad, scared, or thankful. Show them that Allah is always listening.

If you want to learn more about Islamic discipline, read our guide on Islamic Discipline Techniques for Children.

4. Use Stories

Share stories of Prophets making dua — Prophet Yunus in the whale, Prophet Musa asking for help. These stories make dua feel real and powerful.

5. Celebrate Answered Duas

When you see a dua being answered, celebrate it. Say "Alhamdulillah, Allah heard our dua!" This builds trust in Allah.

You may also like our guide on How to Talk to Kids About Allah.

🤲 Dua: "Rabbana taqabbal minna..." — "Our Lord, accept from us..." (Surah Al-Baqarah 2:127)

Final Thoughts

Making dua fun helps children build a lifelong connection with Allah. Keep it simple, joyful, and consistent. May your children always feel close to Allah through dua, in sha Allah.
