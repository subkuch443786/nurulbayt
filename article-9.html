Islamic Bedtime Routine for Kids

By NurulBayt · 6 min read

A calm and consistent bedtime routine is essential for every child — and when that routine is rooted in Islamic values, it becomes a source of blessings and peace.

Here is a step-by-step Islamic bedtime routine that will help your child end the day with faith, love, and tranquility.

1. Evening Adhkar

Before sleeping, recite the evening adhkar together. Teach your child to say: "Bismillah, tawakkaltu 'alallah".

💡 Tip: Recite "A'udhu bi kalimatillah..." — "I seek refuge in the perfect words of Allah."

2. Wudhu Before Bed

The Prophet ﷺ recommended performing wudhu before sleeping. It purifies the body and soul.

3. Brush Teeth (Miswak/Siwak)

Oral hygiene is part of the Sunnah. Encourage your child to brush their teeth or use a miswak.

4. Read a Bedtime Story

Choose Islamic stories — from the lives of the Prophets, Sahaba, or simple moral tales.

5. Recite Quran Together

Recite a few verses of the Quran together — Surah Al-Fatihah, Surah Al-Ikhlas, Surah Al-Falaq, and Surah An-Nas.

6. Make Dua for Protection

Teach your child to recite the dua before sleeping: "Bismika Rabbi wada'tu janbi wa bika arfa'uhu..."

7. Cuddle and Talk

End the routine with a warm hug and a few gentle words. Ask your child: "What are you grateful for today?"

🤲 Dua: "Allahumma aslamtu nafsi ilayka..." (Bukhari)

Final Thoughts

An Islamic bedtime routine is a way to nurture your child's soul, deepen their connection to Allah, and build a lasting bond with you. May Allah bless your home with peace and tranquility, in sha Allah.
