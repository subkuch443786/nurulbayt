How To Raise Children Who Love Allah

Tarbiyah ✍️ By NurulBayt June 2026 ⏱ 7 min read

1. Make Allah Real to Them

Talk about Allah in everyday life. Mention His beautiful attributes — Al-Rahman (The Most Merciful), Al-Wadud (The Loving). Let your child see Allah's blessings in the food they eat, the sky they see, and the love they feel in your home.

💡 Tip: Say "Alhamdulillah" together when you eat. Say "Masha'Allah" when you see something beautiful. Make Allah a natural part of daily conversation.

2. Show Them Islamic Values Through Your Actions

Children learn more from what they see than what they hear. Be honest, kind, patient, and forgiving. When they see you praying with love, they will learn to love Salah. When they see you helping others, they will learn compassion.

3. Make Dua for Them Consistently

The Prophet ﷺ said: "Three supplications are answered without doubt: the supplication of the oppressed, the supplication of the traveler, and the supplication of the parent for their child." (Tirmidhi). Never underestimate the power of a parent's dua.

🤲 Dua: "Rabbana hab lana min azwajina wa dhurriyyatina qurrata a'yunin." — 'Our Lord, grant us from among our spouses and offspring comfort to our eyes.' (Surah Al-Furqan 25:74)

4. Create a Loving Islamic Environment

Fill your home with the remembrance of Allah. Play Quran recitations softly. Read Islamic stories before bedtime. Surround your children with good company — friends who remind them of Allah.

5. Teach Them Who Allah Is

Teach them the 99 names of Allah in simple, beautiful ways. Allah is Al-Ghafoor (The Forgiving) — when they make mistakes, remind them Allah forgives. Allah is Al-Razzaq (The Provider) — teach them to thank Allah for every blessing.

Final Thoughts

Raising children who love Allah is a journey of love, patience, and consistency. Start small. Be consistent. And always trust Allah's plan. Your efforts are never wasted — they are seeds that will bloom in this life and the next, in sha Allah.
