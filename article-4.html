10 Daily Habits Of Righteous Muslim Children

By NurulBayt · 7 min read

Righteousness is not built overnight — it is built through daily habits. The Prophet Muhammad ﷺ was consistent in his actions, and that consistency is what shaped his beautiful character.

Here are 10 simple daily habits, inspired by the Quran and Sunnah, that can help your child develop strong faith and good character.

1. Morning Adhkar

Start the day with the remembrance of Allah. Teach your child to say: "Bismillah" before everything, and "Alhamdulillah" upon waking.

2. Prayer (Salah)

Make prayer a natural part of their day. Even if they are young, invite them to join you.

3. Reading Quran

Set aside 10-15 minutes daily for Quran reading. Even one page a day is a blessing.

💡 Tip: Ask your child every night: "What are you grateful for today?"

4. Gratitude

Teach them to say "Alhamdulillah" for everything. Gratitude opens doors to more blessings.

If you want to learn more about Islamic discipline, read our guide on Islamic Discipline Techniques for Children.

5. Kindness to Others

Encourage them to share, help, and speak kindly. The Prophet ﷺ said: "The best of people are those who are most beneficial to others."

6. Honesty

Teach them that truthfulness leads to righteousness. Praise them when they tell the truth.

7. Helping Parents

Encourage them to help with small chores — setting the table, tidying their room.

8. Evening Adhkar

End the day with the remembrance of Allah. Teach them to recite: "Bismillah, tawakkaltu 'alallah" before sleeping.

You may also like our guide on How to Talk to Kids About Allah.

9. Dua for Parents

Teach them to make dua for their parents. This instills love, respect, and empathy.

10. Self-Reflection

Before sleeping, ask them: "What was one good thing you did today?"

🤲 Dua: "Rabbana hab lana min azwajina wa dhurriyyatina qurrata a'yunin..." (Surah Al-Furqan 25:74)

Final Thoughts

Small habits, done consistently, lead to big results. May Allah make them among the righteous, in sha Allah.
