10 Daily Habits Of Righteous Muslim Children

Habits ✍️ By NurulBayt June 2026 ⏱ 5 min read

1. Morning Adhkar — Start the Day with Allah

Teach children to begin every morning with simple adhkar (remembrance). 'Bismillah' before every action, 'Alhamdulillah' for waking up, and the morning dua for protection. A day started with Allah's name carries barakah throughout.

🌅 Morning Dua: "Allahumma bika asbahna wa bika amsayna wa bika nahya wa bika namutu wa ilaikan-nushoor." — 'O Allah, by You we enter the morning, by You we enter the evening, by You we live, by You we die, and to You is the resurrection.'

2. Salah — Praying on Time

Encourage children to pray their five daily prayers on time. Start with two prayers for younger children and build up gradually. Praying on time teaches discipline, connection with Allah, and a rhythmic structure to the day.

3. Quran Time — Daily Reading or Listening

Even five minutes of daily Quran — reading, listening, or memorising — creates a profound connection with the Book of Allah. Make it a fixed part of the daily routine, perhaps after Fajr or before bed.

📖 The Prophet ﷺ said: "The best of you are those who learn the Quran and teach it." (Bukhari)

4. Saying Bismillah Before Every Action

Teach children to say Bismillah before eating, drinking, studying, and starting any task. This simple habit transforms everyday actions into acts of worship and keeps the remembrance of Allah constant.

5. Helping at Home — Serving the Family

The Prophet ﷺ used to help with household chores. Assigning children small responsibilities — tidying their room, helping with dishes — builds character, gratitude, and humility.

6. Evening Adhkar — End the Day with Gratitude

Before sleeping, teach children to say Ayat al-Kursi, the three Quls, and blow on their hands to recite over themselves. This nightly habit builds a sense of spiritual protection and closeness to Allah.

7. Saying Alhamdulillah for Everything

Train children to express gratitude constantly — for their food, their health, their family, and even their challenges. Gratitude is one of the highest stations in Islam and one of the most powerful habits for a joyful life.

8. Kindness to Others — Living the Sunnah

Encourage children to perform small acts of kindness daily — smiling, sharing, helping a sibling. The Prophet ﷺ said even a smile is sadaqah. Kindness practiced daily becomes a core part of their character.

9. Making Dua — Talking to Allah

Teach children to make dua for their needs, worries, and wishes. When children learn to take everything to Allah — big and small — they develop a trust and reliance on Him that will sustain them through all of life's challenges.

10. Learning Something New About Islam Daily

A small Islamic fact, a new name of Allah, a Sunnah they did not know — learning something new about their deen every day keeps curiosity alive and builds a lifelong love of Islamic knowledge.

🌟 Remember: Habits are not built overnight. Be patient. Celebrate small wins. Consistency over perfection is the key to lasting righteous character in your child.

Final Thoughts

These ten habits do not require perfection — they require consistency and love. Pick one or two to start, establish them firmly, then add more. Each habit is a seed of righteousness that will flourish throughout your child's life, in sha Allah.
